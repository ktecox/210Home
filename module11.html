Module 11

Personally, my SCRUM group did not provide me much advice. I had a very set vision, and I knew exactly what I wanted my page to look like—lots of pink, and very, very simple. They overall liked my color scheme, and felt that I had a grasp on the design of my website. The one area in which they really directed the future of my portfolio was in eliminating one of the pages. I originally had five different pages on my website: home, about, portfolio, resume, and contact. My group helped me realize that I am not that complex of a person, and that really, my about page was merely a repetition of my home page, and due to their advice, I cut it out, something that then caused its own fair share of problems with realignment.

However, I felt as though I helped my group members, and was hopefully not too harsh with my suggestions. One of my group members, Ian, had some serious struggles with his original color scheme, and I think the advice I gave him about sticking with a palate that matched his pirate flag was helpful, or at least I hope it was. Another group member of mine, Chris was stubborn about text overlapping an image on his landing page, but he eventually moved it over.

I thought that portfolio days in general were very helpful. I would use those times as a time to get serious work done on my portfolio, something that I was not very good at apart from those times. These days were also useful because I had lots and lots of questions that I could not figure out, and Google wasn’t too helpful, but I could ask you, and usually figure it out, which was much better than sitting confused in my dorm room.
